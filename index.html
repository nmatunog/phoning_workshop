Master Your Time, Master Your Success

Welcome! This workshop is designed to help you master the critical skills of calendar management and effective phoning. Time is your most valuable asset as an advisor. By learning to manage it with intentionality, you can unlock your full potential.

This module is based on the principles from "Bridge Your Gap" by Jim Effner.

The 8 Steps to Mastery

Step 1: Create Your Ideal Calendar

Master your calendar with an abundance mentality. Your goal isn't just to be busy, but to see the right people. Fill your calendar with intentionality and purpose.

  • Scarcity vs. Abundance: If you believe in your value and operate in a populated market, your issue isn't seeing enough people, it's seeing the right ones.
  • Be Intentional: You want the right kind of appointment, at the right time, on the right day, and in the right place.
  • Learn to Say "No": Think about opportunity cost. Every time you say "yes" to a less-than-ideal appointment, you are saying "no" to a potentially better one.

Step 2: Relentlessly Fill Appointment Slots

The ideal calendar concept is like running a hotel business. One of the most crucial elements to manage is having all rooms booked every night. Every empty room is a cost or a lost opportunity. View your calendar the same way.

Take time to strategize and do work with much intention or purpose. Aim for at least 60% of your day to be "billable hours."

"Every timeslot that is vacant is a lost opportunity!"

Activity: Calculate Your "Billable Hours"

It is important to be aware of your “billable hours”—this is how much time you spend working for a client. Let’s compute your billable hours.

Billable Hour Formula:

  • 1 face-to-face meeting = 1 billable hour
  • 30 phone calls made = 1 billable hour

Instructions:

  • Tally the time you leave the house and the time you get home this week.
  • Add up all your “billable hours” based on the formula above.
  • Remember: Do not include travel time in your billable hours.

Example Scenario:

For the day, Jim left the house at 8:30am and came home at 7:30pm. He made 30 phone calls and had one 2-hour meeting, but another was postponed. He also spent 1.5 hours travelling.

  • Billable Hours: 2 hours (1 hr for 30 calls + 1 hr for the meeting)
  • Total Hours Worked: 11 hours
  • Only 18% of Jim’s day was actually “paid”.

Now, discover how much of your day is truly productive. The goal is to have at least 60% of your day consist of "billable hours."

Step 3: Don't Compromise Your Plan

The hardest part of calendar management is living it. Setting it up is easy; implementation is hard. Define your "sacred times" for phoning, planning, or personal life, and protect them fiercely.

What are the "sacred times" of your week? Identify and honor them.

Step 4: Honor Your Phoning Time

Treat your scheduled phoning time as if it were an appointment with your most important client. If a client asks to meet during your phoning block, offer a different time. Do not budge.

"Cancelling your phoning time psychologically tells you that it is not important."

Step 5: Use Identical Phoning Language

Design a script that is authentic, effective, and laser-focused on getting the appointment. Practice it until it sounds natural, not like a telemarketer.

Always end by offering two specific time slots that need to be filled.

Step 6: Memorize Objection Responses

You will encounter the same few objections repeatedly (e.g., "I'm not interested," "I'm too busy," "I have an advisor"). Prepare and memorize your responses so that they never throw you off your game. Confidence comes from preparation.

Step 7: Go Through Three Objections

People are often programmed to say "no" the first time. A successful advisor doesn't give up easily. Aim to work through three objections with every client.

If they give a third objection, say:

"Mr. Prospect, things have a way of changing over time. Would you have any objection if I kept in touch with you every 6 months?"

Step 8: Stand Up While Phoning

Your energy is contagious. Standing up improves your volume, tone, and inflection. Let your passion and smile jump through the phone and grab the prospect's attention. Even though they can't see you, they can feel your confidence.
